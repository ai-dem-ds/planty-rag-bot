Title: Rosemary
Source: Kew
URL: https://www.kew.org/plants/rosemary

Scientific name: Salvia Rosmarinus
Common name: Rosemary
Family: Lamiaceae

Description:
Rosemary is an aromatic, sun-loving evergreen herb in the mint family. It has narrow needle-like leaves and two-lipped, purplish-blue and white flowers and a strong fragrance. It is widely used in gardens and kitchens.

Plat uses
1. Beauty and cosmetics:
	- Rosemary flowers and leaves contain an essential oil which has a strong aromatic fragrance, making it a popular ingredient in perfumery, cosmetics, hair products and incense burners.
	- Rosemary has strong antioxidant properties and contains iron, calcium, and phytonutrients so it is added to hair care products to provide hydration and protection from sun damage.
2. Cultural:
	- Rosemarys pretty, purple flowers often feature in ornamental garden displays. It thrives in containers and along sunny borders in dry regions.
3. Food and drink:
	- Rosemary is one of the most prized herbs used in cooking, especially in Mediterranean cuisine.
	- It is used to flavor various dishes, both sweet and savory, but is most popular in dressings for meats. The leaves have a bitter taste which compliments fatty foods like lamb and oily fish.
	- Narbonne honey from France mostly comes from bees feeding on rosemary blossoms.
4. Health:
	- The leaves and flowers of rosemary can be used to make a tea, thought to relieve headaches, colic, colds, and depression.
	- Rosemary also has antibacterial and antifungal properties.
	- It was used in traditional European herbal remedies for a variety of ailments, including wounds, eczema, poor appetite, and asthma.
Rosemary is used as a culinary herb, an ornamental garden plant, and in fragrant products. It is often grown in herb gardens and Mediterranean-style plantings.

Related plants:
Rosemary belongs to the mint family, which also includes lavender, mint, and sage.

Growing conditions:
Rosemary usually prefers full sun and well-drained soil. It can tolerate dry conditions better than many soft-leaved herbs.

Seasonality
Best time to see: 
	- Flowers: Jun, Jul, Aug, Sep, Oct

Distribution
Where in the world:
	- Native: Albania, Algeria, Baleares, Bosnia and Herzegovina, Corse, Croatia, Cyprus, East Aegean Islands, Egypt, France, Greece, Italy, Libya, Montenegro, Morocco, North Macedonia, Portugal, Sardinia, Serbia, Sicily, Slovenia, Spain, Tunisia, Türkiye
	- Introduced: Azores, Bermuda, Bulgaria, Canary Islands, Cape Verde, Crimea, Great Britain, Kriti, Madeira, Mexico Central, Mexico Southwest, Texas, Trinidad-Tobago

Notes:
Rosemary is valued for both practical use in cooking and decorative use in gardens.
