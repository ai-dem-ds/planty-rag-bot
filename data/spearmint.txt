Title: Spearmint
Source: Kew
URL: https://www.kew.org/plants/spearmint

Scientific name: Mentha Spicata
Common name: Mint
Family: Lamiaceae

Description:
Spearmint is a well-known aromatic herb with erect, square-shaped stems and narrow, oval-shaped leaves that have pointed tips and toothed edges. The leaves are arranged in opposite pairs and the small, pink, light purple or white flowers appear in spikes. It is valued for its fresh scent and fast growth. Many types of mint are used in food, drinks, and home gardens.

Plant uses:
1. Beauty and cosmetics:
	- The aromatic oil from the spearmint plant is sometimes used to fragrance perfumes, shampoos, and soaps.
2. Food and drink:
	- Spearmint leaves add a fresh taste to several drinks including mojitos and herbal teas. They are also used as a garnish and are sometimes added to salads.
	- Spearmint oil is used to flavor toothpaste and confectionary
3. Health:
	- In Colombia, the stems and leaves of the spearmint plant are used to relieve stomach aches.
Mint is commonly used in teas, drinks, desserts, and savory dishes. It is also used for fragrance and general herb gardening.

Related plants:
Mint belongs to the same family as rosemary, lavender, and sage.

Growing conditions:
Mint often grows well in moist soil and can spread quickly. It is commonly grown in containers or controlled garden beds because it can become invasive.

Seasonality:
	- Spring: starts growing
	- Summer: peak growth & best harvest
	- Autumn: slows down
	- Winter: dormant (dies back above ground)
In southern Germany: grows ~April - October, best in summer.
