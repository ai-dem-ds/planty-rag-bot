Title: Chamomile
Source: Kew
URL: https://www.kew.org/plants/chamomile

Scientific name: Chamaemelum nobile
Common name: Chamomile
Family: Asteraceae

Description:
Chamomile is a charming, ground-hugging herb that releases a beautiful, apple-like scent when crushed.
Commonly found in herbal teas and infusions, chamomile can aid relaxation and sleep. It was also traditionally used as a natural remedy for fevers, headaches, and indigestion.
Chamomile is a low-growing, aromatic herb with finely divided green leaves and white daisy-like flowers with yellow centers.

Plant uses
1. Beauty and cosmetics:
- Chamomile has a long tradition in hair care as it was used to enhance the color of blonde hair and to make hair shine.
- It is currently used in some skin-balms because of its soothing properties, and in shampoos.

2. Food and drink:
- Chamomile is most used as an ingredient of herbal teas and has also been used to aromatize traditional liqueurs. Commonly used to make herbal tea.

2. Health:
- Chamomile was used as a traditional remedy for toothache, stomach ache, and headaches. It was also used as a relaxant and to calm the stomach.
- Traditionally valued for calming and soothing uses.
- Today, a chamomile infusion is often taken to reduce stress and aid sleep.

3. Ornamental:
- Grown in herb gardens and cottage-style planting.

Growing conditions:
- Suited to sunny locations.
- Generally prefers well-drained soil.
- Mown or closely grazed, mildly acidic grassland in open places, such as sandy heaths or coastal cliff tops.

Seasonality
Best time to see: 
- Foliage: Apr, May, Jun, Jul, Aug, Sep, Oct

Distribution
- Native: Native: Algeria, Azores, Great Britain, Madeira, Morocco, Portugal, Spain, Transcaucasia
- Introduced: Albania, Austria, Baltic States, Belarus, Belgium, Bosnia and Herzegovina, Bulgaria, California, Central European Russia, Colombia, Connecticut, Crimea, Croatia, Czech Republic, Delaware, France, Germany, Haiti, Illinois, Indiana, Ireland, Italy, Maryland, Montenegro, New Jersey, New York, North Carolina, North Macedonia, Ohio, Pakistan, Poland, Serbia, Slovakia, Slovenia, South Australia, Switzerland, Tasmania, Ukraine, Victoria, Wisconsin

Notes:
Chamomile is valued for fragrance, herbal tea use, and its soft, daisy-like appearance.
The word chamomile and the genus name Chamaemelum come from the Greek word for ‘earth-apple’ because of the apple-like scent of the plant.
