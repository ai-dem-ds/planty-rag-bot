Title: Grand Mesa Beardtongue
Source: Kew
URL: https://www.kew.org/plants/grand-mesa-beardtongue

Scientific name: Penstemon mensarum
Common name: Grand mesa beardtongue
Family: Plantaginaceae

Description:
Grand mesa beardtongue is a rare flowering plant known for its striking blue flowers.
The striking blue beauty, grand mesa beardtongue, is a rare plant found only in Colorado.
It's rarity comes from the threats its faces from road maintenance activities and invasive weeds so it needs protecting. 
Grand mesa beardtongue has intense blue flowers with hints of purple to pink that appear on upright stems with evergreen leaves.

Plant uses:
1. Conservation importance:
- This plant is notable more for its rarity and conservation value than for practical human use.

Conservation:
- It is found only in western Colorado.
- Kew notes that it is known from only a small number of locations.
- Major threats include road maintenance activity and invasive weeds.

Growing conditions:
- Associated with its restricted Colorado habitat.

Distribution:
Native range: western Colorado, USA.
